The Story

Built from Nothing.
Sold to the Top.

Chad Panton didn't inherit a playbook. He wrote one. Starting in 2007, he built JavaWorks from the ground up — a market-leading office coffee, water filtration, and micro-market services company that served the Greater Tampa and Orlando markets for over 14 years.

In December 2021, Chad sold JavaWorks to a Fortune 50 company — a landmark exit that proved his model, validated his leadership, and opened the door to an entirely new chapter.

But he didn't cash out and cruise. He heard a different call. After the sale, God opened a door for Chad to travel to Costa Rica — to be still, to listen, and to let the next chapter take shape. Out of that stillness, he founded In La Keshe — a Mayan phrase meaning "Oneness with God" — and returned not as a man who had arrived, but as a man who had just begun.

Today, Chad operates at the intersection of faith, business, and purpose — stewarding a portfolio of ventures, investments, and real estate across the U.S. and internationally, always with one question at the center: Does this glorify God?

The Health Journey

The Body Keeps
the Score.

This story doesn't get told enough. Chad Panton — high-performing entrepreneur, serial investor, man of faith — moved into a home that passed inspection. What the inspection missed was quietly devastating: severe mold contamination in an undisclosed area of the home.

The toxins built. His cognition clouded. His energy collapsed. His emotional state shifted in ways that didn't make sense. And Western medicine handed him a prescription. He refused it.

What followed was years of testing, seeking, and suffering — eventually uncovering a cascade of co-infections including Lyme disease, heavy metal toxicity, parasites, Epstein-Barr, and chronic fatigue, all compounded by a leaky gut that had been silently blocking every supplement he took from being absorbed.

Now, Chad is healing. Armed with a sequenced functional protocol, renewed faith, and the conviction that God is using this story for something greater — he is reclaiming his mind, his body, and his calling.
