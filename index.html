The Story

Built from the Ground Up.
Sold to the Top.

Chad Panton didn't inherit a playbook. He wrote one. Starting with nothing more than a vision and relentless work ethic, he built JavaWorks — a market-leading office coffee, water filtration, and micro-market services company serving the Tampa Bay and Orlando markets for over 14 years.

In December 2021, Chad sold JavaWorks to a Fortune 50 company — a milestone that validated everything he had built and everything he believed. But more than the exit, it was what came next that defines who Chad Panton is.

He heard a different call. After the sale, God opened a door for Chad to travel to Costa Rica — to be still, to listen, and to let the next chapter take shape. Out of that season of quiet, he founded a Costa Rican corporation named In La Keshe — a Mayan phrase meaning "Oneness with God" — and returned home not as a man who had arrived, but as a man who had just begun.

Today, Chad operates at the intersection of faith, business, and purpose — stewarding a portfolio of ventures, investments, and real estate across the United States and internationally, always with one question at the center: Does this glorify God?

The Health Journey

The Body Keeps
the Score.

This story doesn't get told enough. Chad Panton — high-performing entrepreneur, serial investor, man of faith — moved into a home that passed inspection. What the inspection missed was quietly devastating: severe mold contamination hidden in an undisclosed area.

Over time, the toxins built. His cognition clouded. His energy collapsed. His emotional state shifted in ways that didn't make sense. And Western medicine handed him a prescription. He refused it.

What followed was years of testing, seeking, suffering, and learning — eventually uncovering a cascade of co-infections including Lyme disease, heavy metal toxicity, parasites, Epstein-Barr, and chronic fatigue, all compounded by a leaky gut that had been quietly blocking every supplement he took from being absorbed.

Now, Chad is healing. Armed with a sequenced protocol, a renewed faith, and a conviction that God is using this story for something greater — he is reclaiming his mind, his body, and his calling.
