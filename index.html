The Story

Built from Scratch.
Sold to the Top.

Chad Panton didn't inherit a playbook. He wrote one. Starting with an idea and relentless work ethic, he built JavaWorks — a market-leading office coffee, water filtration, and micro-market services company serving Tampa Bay and Orlando for over 14 years.

In December 2021, Chad sold JavaWorks to a Fortune 50 company — a landmark exit that validated everything he built and everything he believed. But more than the number, it was what came next that defines who Chad Panton truly is.

He heard a different call. After the sale, God opened a door for Chad to travel to Costa Rica — to be still, to listen. He founded a corporation named In La Keshe — a Mayan phrase meaning "Oneness with God" — and returned not as a man who had arrived, but as a man who had just begun.

Today, Chad operates at the intersection of faith, business, and purpose — stewarding a portfolio of ventures, investments, and real estate across the US and internationally, always asking: Does this glorify God?

The Health Journey

The Body Keeps
the Score.

This story doesn't get told enough. Chad Panton — high-performing entrepreneur, serial investor, man of faith — moved into a home that passed inspection. What the inspection missed was quietly devastating: severe mold contamination hidden in an undisclosed area.

Over time, the toxins built. His cognition clouded. His energy collapsed. His emotional state shifted in ways that didn't make sense. And Western medicine handed him a prescription. He refused it.

What followed was years of testing, seeking, and learning — eventually uncovering a cascade of co-infections including Lyme disease, heavy metal toxicity, parasites, and Epstein-Barr, all compounded by a leaky gut blocking every supplement he took from being absorbed.

Now, Chad is healing — armed with a sequenced protocol, a renewed faith, and the conviction that God is using this story for something greater.
